Social graph

A graph normally draws people as identical dots and connections as identical lines. But real people aren't identical — and neither is what happens when one of them gets close.

Drag the circles. Or leave them alone — watch who drifts toward whom.

Pull two friends close to a third. Both bonds glow more than either alone — that's synergy. Being around two people you love isn't twice as good as one. It's more.

Now watch a neglected bond thin over time. Relationships fade without contact. Pull them back together and the bond heals.

Leave the graph alone and it self-organizes: clusters form around strong bonds, draining connections push people away, and weak ties stretch thin at the edges.
